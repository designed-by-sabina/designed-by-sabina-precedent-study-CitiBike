CitiBike Rebalancing Study_

An investigation into ways to rebalance CitiBike stations throughout New York City
by Center for Spatial Research at Columbia University

layers of analysis

ontological analysis_
The study processes the following input parameters: date and time, location of Citi Bike docks, capacity (number of docks) at each location, number of available bikes at each location, number of empty docks at each location, origin and destination stations of each bike trip, and departure and arrival times. Diagrams illustrate the number of arrivals/departures at each location at a specific time. The author was particularly interested in recording morning and evening commute patterns to assist in the development of rebalancing strategies. To determine the stations in need of rebalancing, researchers defined full stations as those that are at least 80% full and empty stations as those that are less than 20% full.

visual language analysis_
The CitiBike Rebalancing Study primarily consists of computational diagrams. In it, you can find heatmaps that show various degrees of concentration of Citi Bikes and their movement. Heatmaps emphasize the flow of data and highlight locations that attract riders. To simplify data search, the author provides matrices as an instrument to track the number of bikes in each location at different times. Both matrices and heatmaps are created in dark mode data visualization and glowing data aesthetics, rooted in scientific visualization. The researchers used ArcGIS, Adobe Illustrator, and Processing to create high-contrast, system-oriented visuals.
